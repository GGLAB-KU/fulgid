1. Define "magma" as melted rock inside the Earth.
2. Define "lava" as magma that has erupted onto Earth's surface.
3. Define "minerals" as the substances that form when magma and lava cool.
4. Identify places inside Earth where rock will melt.
5. If magma forms, determine if it will reach the surface.
6. If magma does not reach the surface, it remains inside the Earth.
7. If the magma is not pushed forward, it may cool and solidify.
8. If the magma is pushed forward, it may eventually reach the surface and become lava.
9. As lava flows from volcanoes, it begins to cool.
10. When magma and lava cool, minerals form.
