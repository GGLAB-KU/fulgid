1. Input sugar into the body
2. Sugar enters the bloodstream
3. The pancreas detects high sugar levels in the bloodstream
4. The pancreas releases insulin to lower sugar levels
5. Insulin travels through the bloodstream to reach the cells
6. Insulin signals the cells to absorb sugar from the bloodstream
7. Blood sugar levels decrease
8. If blood sugar levels remain elevated, the pancreas detects the need for more regulation
9. The pancreas releases additional insulin to further lower sugar levels
10. Insulin travels through the bloodstream to reach the cells
11. Insulin signals the cells to absorb more sugar from the bloodstream
12. Blood sugar levels decrease further
13. The pancreas continues to monitor blood sugar levels and adjusts insulin production as needed to maintain normal levels.
