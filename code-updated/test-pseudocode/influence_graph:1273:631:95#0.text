1. Define a flowering plant with stamen and pistils.
2. Wait for an insect or bird to land on the plant.
3. When the insect or bird lands, some of the pollen sticks to it.
4. The insect or bird attempts to travel to another plant of the same species.
5. If the insect or bird is successful in reaching another plant, the pollen is transferred from the insect or bird to the new plant.
6. If the insect or bird is eaten before it reaches another plant, the pollen transfer is unsuccessful and fewer flowers are pollinated.
